2. Security Audit: 
To view the value, run: kubectl get secret ml-auth -o jsonpath='{.data.API_KEY}'
The value is NOT encrypted; it is only Base64 encoded. Anyone with access to the cluster can easily decode it (e.g., using [System.Text.Encoding]::UTF8.GetString([System.Convert]::FromBase64String('YOUR_ENCODED_STRING')) in PowerShell).

The 'Stabilization Window' is a setting that prevents the HPA from scaling up or down too rapidly (flapping). By default, it looks at metrics over a specific timeframe to ensure the load is consistent before making a scaling decision.

4. Fault Tolerance: 
When you delete a pod, Kubernetes immediately notices the 'Actual State' (0 pods) does not match your 'Desired State' (1 pod in the Deployment). It immediately creates a new pod to restore the balance. This is the core 'Self-Healing' nature of Kubernetes.
